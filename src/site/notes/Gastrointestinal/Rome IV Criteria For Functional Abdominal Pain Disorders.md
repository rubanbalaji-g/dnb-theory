## Core Diagnostic Principles

- Diagnosis requires symptoms unexplained by alternative medical conditions following appropriate medical evaluation.
- Prior criteria mandated absolute absence of inflammatory, anatomic, metabolic, or neoplastic processes.
- Updated criteria reduce requirement for intensive, invasive exclusionary testing.
- Pathologies classified under overarching category of disorders of gut-brain interaction.

## Diagnostic Criteria By Specific Subtype

### Irritable Bowel Syndrome

- Criteria fulfilled for minimum 2 months prior to diagnosis.
- Abdominal pain occurs minimum 4 days per month.
- Pain associates with minimum one specific defecation characteristic.
- Defecation characteristics include relation to defecation, altered stool frequency, altered stool form or appearance.
- Pain persists despite resolution of concurrent constipation.

### Functional Dyspepsia

- Criteria fulfilled for minimum 2 months prior to diagnosis.
- Bothersome symptoms occur minimum 4 days per month.
- Core symptoms include postprandial fullness, early satiation, epigastric pain, or epigastric burning.
- Pain lacks association with defecation.
- Subdivided into postprandial distress syndrome and epigastric pain syndrome.

### Abdominal Migraine

- Criteria fulfilled for minimum 6 months prior to diagnosis.
- Minimum two paroxysmal episodes of intense, acute periumbilical, midline, or diffuse pain.
- Pain duration strictly exceeds 1 hour.
- Episodes separated by weeks to months.
- Pain causes profound incapacitation, interfering with normal activities.
- Exhibits stereotypical pattern in individual patient.
- Pain associates with minimum two secondary features.
- Secondary features include anorexia, nausea, vomiting, headache, photophobia, pallor.

### Functional Abdominal Pain Not Otherwise Specified

- Symptoms persist for 2 to 6 months.
- Pain occurs minimum 4 times per month.
- Features episodic or continuous abdominal pain.
- Pain lacks association with physiologic events including eating or menses.
- Exhibits insufficient criteria for irritable bowel syndrome, functional dyspepsia, or abdominal migraine.

|Disorder Category|Frequency Requirement|Minimum Duration|Defining Clinical Features|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|Irritable bowel syndrome|$\ge$ 4 days/month|2 months|Pain related to defecation, altered stool frequency, or altered stool form.|
|Functional dyspepsia|$\ge$ 4 days/month|2 months|Postprandial fullness, early satiation, or epigastric pain/burning.|
|Abdominal migraine|$\ge$ 2 discrete episodes|6 months|Intense paroxysmal periumbilical/midline pain lasting $\ge$ 1 hour; associated vasomotor/somatic signs.|
