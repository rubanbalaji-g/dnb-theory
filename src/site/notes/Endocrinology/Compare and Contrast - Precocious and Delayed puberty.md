## Precocious puberty

### Central Precocious Puberty (CPP)

| <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Etiology Category</span> | <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Male Manifestations</span>                                                                                                                                                                                                         | <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Female Manifestations</span>                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| :------------------------------------------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Idiopathic**                                                | <li>Less common.</li>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   | <li>Most common etiology.</li><li>Accounts for 90-95% of cases.</li>                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **CNS / <br>Congenital Lesions**                              | <li>Acquired:<ul><li>Severe head trauma</li><li>Postencephalitic scars</li><li>Tuberculous meningitis</li><li>Brain abscesses</li><li>CNS radiation</li></ul></li><li>Congenital:<ul><li>Hydrocephalus</li><li>Arachnoid cysts</li><li>Suprasellar cysts</li></ul></li> | <li>Acquired:<ul><li>Severe head trauma</li><li>Postencephalitic scars</li><li>Tuberculous meningitis</li><li>Brain abscesses</li><li>CNS radiation</li></ul></li><li>Congenital:<ul><li>Hydrocephalus</li><li>Arachnoid cysts</li><li>Suprasellar cysts</li></ul></li> |
| **Genetic / Monogenic Causes**                                | <li>Pathogenic variants in:<ul><li>MKRN3</li><li>DLK1</li><li>KISS1</li><li>KISS1R/GPR54</li></ul></li>                                                                                                                                                                 | <li>Pathogenic variants in:<ul><li>MKRN3</li><li>DLK1</li><li>KISS1</li><li>KISS1R/GPR54</li></ul></li>                                                                                                                                                                 |

### Peripheral Precocious Puberty (PPP)

| <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Etiology Category</span>               | <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Male Manifestations</span>                                                                                            | <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Female Manifestations</span>                                                                                                                                                          |
| :-------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| <span style="color:rgb(146, 208, 80)">Gonadal Pathology</span>              | <li>Leydig cell tumors</li><li>hCG-secreting tumors:<ul><li>germinomas</li><li>chorioepitheliomas</li><li>teratomas</li><li>hepatoblastomas</li></ul></li> | <li>Autonomous estrogen-secreting ovarian follicular cysts</li><li>Ovarian neoplasms:<ul><li>juvenile granulosa cell tumors</li><li>thecomas</li><li>luteomas</li><li>dysgerminomas</li><li>gonadoblastomas</li></ul></li> |
| **<span style="color:rgb(146, 208, 80)">Adrenal Pathology</span>**          | <li>Virilizing Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH)</li><li>Virilizing adrenal adenomas/carcinomas</li>                                                    | <li>Feminizing adrenal tumors:<ul><li>isosexual</li></ul></li><li>Virilizing adrenal tumors/classic CAH:<ul><li>contrasexual</li></ul></li>                                                                                |
| **<span style="color:rgb(146, 208, 80)">Syndromic & Genetic Causes</span>** | <li>Familial male-limited precocious puberty (Testotoxicosis)</li><li>McCune-Albright syndrome</li>                                                        | <li>McCune-Albright syndrome</li><li>Aromatase Excess Syndrome</li>                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Severe Primary Hypothyroidism**                                           | <li>Van Wyk-Grumbach Syndrome (macroorchidism).</li>                                                                                                       | <li>Van Wyk-Grumbach Syndrome (multicystic ovaries, breast enlargement, galactorrhea).</li>                                                                                                                                |
| **<span style="color:rgb(146, 208, 80)">Exogenous Exposure</span>**         | <li>Accidental systemic absorption of testosterone.</li>                                                                                                   | <li>Topical estrogen creams, oral contraceptives, anabolic steroids, environmental phytoestrogens.</li>                                                                                                                    |

## Delayed Puberty

| :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Constitutional Delay of Growth & Puberty (CDGP)**                                                       | <li>Most common male etiology</li><li>65-80% referral cases.</li>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | <li>Most frequent cause overall.</li><li>Diagnosed less frequently than in boys.</li>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| **Functional / Transient Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism**                                                  | <li>Systemic:<ul><li>Celiac</li><li>IBD</li><li>cystic fibrosis (CF)</li><li>sickle cell</li><li>thalassemia</li><li>chronic renal failure</li></ul></li><li>Nutritional:<ul><li>Malnutrition</li><li>severe weight loss</li><li>anorexia nervosa</li><li>excessive physical training</li></ul></li><li>Endocrine:<ul><li>Uncontrolled T1DM</li><li>profound hypothyroidism</li><li>Cushing syndrome</li><li>hyperprolactinemia</li></ul></li> | <li>Systemic:<ul><li>Celiac</li><li>IBD</li><li>cystic fibrosis (CF)</li><li>sickle cell</li><li>thalassemia</li><li>chronic renal failure</li></ul></li><li>Nutritional:<ul><li>Malnutrition</li><li>severe weight loss</li><li>anorexia nervosa</li><li>excessive physical training</li></ul></li><li>Endocrine:<ul><li>Uncontrolled T1DM</li><li>profound hypothyroidism</li><li>Cushing syndrome</li><li>hyperprolactinemia</li></ul></li> |
| **Permanent Central Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism**                                                       | <ul><li>Kallmann syndrome</li><li>Normosmic IHH</li><li>Multiple Pituitary Hormone Deficiencies (MPHD)</li><li>CNS lesions:<ul><li>Craniopharyngiomas</li><li>germinomas</li><li>gliomas</li><li>irradiation</li><li>trauma</li><li>infiltrative diseases</li></ul></li><li>Defects:<ul><li>Septo-optic dysplasia</li></ul></li><li>Syndromes:<ul><li>Prader-Willi</li><li>Laurence-Moon-Biedl</li><li>CHARGE</li></ul></li></ul>              | <ul><li>Kallmann syndrome</li><li>Normosmic IHH</li><li>Multiple Pituitary Hormone Deficiencies (MPHD)</li><li>CNS lesions:<ul><li>Craniopharyngiomas</li><li>germinomas</li><li>gliomas</li><li>irradiation</li><li>trauma</li><li>infiltrative diseases</li></ul></li><li>Defects:<ul><li>Septo-optic dysplasia</li></ul></li><li>Syndromes:<ul><li>Prader-Willi</li><li>Laurence-Moon-Biedl</li><li>CHARGE</li></ul></li></ul>              |
| **<span style="color:rgb(146, 208, 80)">Hypergonadotropic Hypogonadism (Primary Gonadal Failure)</span>** | <li>Klinefelter syndrome (47,XXY) (most common)</li><li>Structural:<ul><li>Congenital anorchia</li><li>cryptorchidism</li><li>Noonan syndrome</li></ul></li><li>Enzymatic:<ul><li>StAR</li><li>CYP17A1</li><li>HSD17B3 mutations</li></ul></li><li>Acquired:<ul><li>Torsion</li><li>trauma</li><li>viral/autoimmune orchitis</li><li>chemotherapy</li><li>irradiation</li></ul></li>                                                           | <li>Turner syndrome (45,X/variants) (most common)</li><li>Dysgenesis:<ul><li>46,XX pure gonadal (Perrault)</li><li>46,XY gonadal (Swyer)</li></ul></li><li>Enzymatic:<ul><li>Aromatase deficiency</li><li>17-alpha-hydroxylase/17,20-lyase deficiency</li><li>Savage syndrome</li></ul></li><li>Acquired:<ul><li>Cytotoxic chemotherapy</li><li>irradiation</li><li>autoimmune oophoritis</li><li>galactosemia</li></ul></li>                  |
| **<span style="color:rgb(146, 208, 80)">Structural / End-Organ Abnormalities (Eugonadism)</span>**        | <li>Not applicable.</li>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | <li>Normal ovarian endocrine function</li><li>Primary amenorrhea via anatomical defects</li><li>Mayer-Rokitansky-Kuster-Hauser syndrome</li><li>Obstructive anomalies:<ul><li>imperforate hymen</li><li>complete transverse vaginal septum</li></ul></li><li>Complete Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome (CAIS)</li>                                                                                                                              |
