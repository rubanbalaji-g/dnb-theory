## Introduction to Dietary Standards

- **Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA):**
    - The RDA is defined as the average daily dietary intake level sufficient to meet the nutrient requirement of nearly all (97% to 98%) healthy individuals in a particular life-stage and gender group.
    - It is calculated as the Estimated Average Requirement (EAR) plus two standard deviations (RDA = EAR + 2SD).
    - RDA is used as the goal for usual intake by an individual.
- **Estimated Average Requirement (EAR):**
    - This is the median daily intake value estimated to meet the requirement of half the healthy individuals in a specific group.
    - It is primarily used to assess the nutrient intake of populations rather than individuals.

## Energy Requirements

Energy requirements vary significantly based on age, growth rate, metabolic rate, and physical activity.

### Infants (0–12 Months)

- **ICMR-NIN 2020 Requirements:**
    - **0–6 months:** 550 kcal/day (approx. 92 kcal/kg/day).
    - **6–12 months:** 670 kcal/day (approx. 80 kcal/kg/day).
    - Energy needs are based on total energy expenditure plus energy deposition for growth.

### Children and Adolescents (ICMR-NIN 2020)

- **Children 1–3 years:** 1060–1110 kcal/day (approx. 82 kcal/kg/day).
- **Children 4–6 years:** 1350 kcal/day.
- **Children 7–9 years:** 1690 kcal/day.
- **Adolescents (Boys):**
    - 10–12 years: 2190 kcal/day.
    - 13–15 years: 2750 kcal/day.
    - 16–18 years: 3020 kcal/day.
- **Adolescents (Girls):**
    - 10–12 years: 2010 kcal/day.
    - 13–15 years: 2330 kcal/day.
    - 16–18 years: 2060 kcal/day.

### Bedside Calculation of Calorie Requirement

- This method is often used by clinicians as a practical guide.
- **0–1 year:** 100 kcal/kg.
- **1 year:** 1000 kcal.
- **>1 year:** 1000 kcal + (100 kcal × each year of age) up to puberty.
- **Puberty:** 2400 kcal (1 unit of energy).

### Holliday and Segar Formula (Fluid and Calorie Estimation)

- Used for maintenance fluid and caloric estimation in well-nourished and hospitalized children.
- **First 10 kg:** 100 kcal/kg.
- **Next 10 kg (11–20 kg):** 1000 kcal + 50 kcal/kg for each kg >10.
- **>20 kg:** 1500 kcal + 20 kcal/kg for each kg >20.

## Protein Requirements

Protein is essential for the synthesis of tissues, enzymes, hormones, and antibodies. Requirements are higher per kilogram in infants due to rapid growth.

### ICMR-NIN 2020 Standards

- **Infants:**
    - 0–6 months: 8.1 g/day.
    - 6–12 months: 10.5 g/day.
- **Children:**
    - 1–3 years: 11.3 g/day.
    - 4–6 years: 15.9 g/day.
    - 7–9 years: 23.3 g/day.
- **Adolescent Boys:**
    - 10–12 years: 31.8 g/day.
    - 13–15 years: 44.9 g/day.
    - 16–18 years: 55.4 g/day.
- **Adolescent Girls:**
    - 10–12 years: 32.8 g/day.
    - 13–15 years: 43.2 g/day.
    - 16–18 years: 46.2 g/day.

## Fat Requirements

- Fats provide energy (9 kcal/g), structural elements for cell membranes, and vehicles for fat-soluble vitamins.
- **Visible Fat RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants (0–6 mo):** 0 g (met by breast milk).
    - **Infants (6–12 mo):** 25 g/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 25–30 g/day.
    - **Adolescents (10–18 years):** 35–50 g/day depending on gender and age.
- **Total Fat Intake:** Should contribute 30–40% of total energy for children 1–3 years and 25–35% for children 4–18 years.
- **Essential Fatty Acids (EFA):** Linoleic acid (3% of energy) and alpha-linolenic acid (0.3% of energy) are required.

## Macronutrient Distribution

- **Carbohydrates:** Should provide 55–60% of total calories.
- **Proteins:** Should provide 10–15% of total calories.
- **Fats:** Should provide 25–30% of total calories.

## Micronutrient Requirements: Fat-Soluble Vitamins

### Vitamin A (Retinol)

- **Functions:** Vision, immune function, growth, and epithelial integrity.
- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants (0–12 mo):** 350 mcg/day.
    - **1–3 years:** 390 mcg/day.
    - **4–6 years:** 510 mcg/day.
    - **7–9 years:** 630 mcg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 770–1000 mcg/day.
- **Bedside Calculation:** ~1500 IU/day (500 mcg).

### Vitamin D (Cholecalciferol)

- **Functions:** Calcium and phosphorus absorption, bone mineralization.
- **RDA (ICMR 2020):** 400 IU (10 mcg) for infants; 600 IU (15 mcg) for children >1 year and adolescents.
- **Note:** Breast milk is deficient in Vitamin D (30–40 IU/L); supplementation is required for exclusively breastfed infants.

### Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

- **Functions:** Antioxidant, immune function.
- **RDA:**
    - **Infants:** 4–5 mg/day.
    - **Children:** 6–11 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 15 mg/day.

### Vitamin K

- **Functions:** Blood clotting, bone metabolism.
- **RDA:**
    - **Infants:** 2.0–2.5 mcg/day.
    - **Children:** 30–55 mcg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 60–75 mcg/day.

## Micronutrient Requirements: Water-Soluble Vitamins

### Thiamine (Vitamin B1)

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 0.2–0.4 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 0.7–1.1 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 1.4–2.2 mg/day.
- **Bedside Calculation:** 0.5–1.5 mg/day (or 1 mg/1000 kcal).

### Riboflavin (Vitamin B2)

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 0.4–0.6 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 0.9–1.6 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 1.9–3.1 mg/day.

### Niacin (Vitamin B3)

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 2–5 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 7–11 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 14–22 mg/day.

### Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6)

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 0.1–0.6 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 0.9–1.5 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 1.9–3.0 mg/day.

### Folic Acid (Vitamin B9)

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 25–85 mcg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 110–170 mcg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 220–340 mcg/day.

### Vitamin B12 (Cyanocobalamin)

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 1.2 mcg/day.
    - **Children/Adolescents:** 1.2–2.5 mcg/day.

### Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 20–27 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 27–43 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 52–82 mg/day.

## Mineral Requirements

### Calcium

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 300 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–3 years):** 500 mg/day.
    - **Children (4–9 years):** 550–650 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents (10–18 years):** 850–1050 mg/day (highest requirement due to bone mass accretion).

### Iron

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants (6–12 mo):** 3 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 8–15 mg/day.
    - **Adolescent Boys:** 16–26 mg/day.
    - **Adolescent Girls:** 28–32 mg/day.

### Zinc

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants (6–12 mo):** 2.5 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 3.0–5.9 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 8.5–17.6 mg/day.

### Magnesium

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 30 mg/day.
    - **Children (1–9 years):** 135–215 mg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 255–405 mg/day.

### Iodine

- **RDA (ICMR 2020):**
    - **Infants:** 100–130 mcg/day.
    - **Children:** 90–120 mcg/day.
    - **Adolescents:** 150 mcg/day.

## Special Considerations: Preterm Infants

Preterm infants have higher metabolic needs and limited stores. The ESPGAN recommendations for preterm RDA are:

- **Energy:** 110–165 kcal/kg/day.
- **Protein:** 3–3.5 g/kg/day.
- **Vitamin A:** 1000 IU/day.
- **Vitamin D:** 400 IU/day.
- **Vitamin E:** 15 IU/day.
- **Calcium:** 100–200 mg/kg/day.
- **Phosphorus:** 50–150 mg/kg/day.
- **Iron:** 2.5 mg/kg/day (starting at 6–8 weeks).
