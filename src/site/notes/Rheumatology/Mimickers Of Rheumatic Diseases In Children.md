## Neoplastic Disorders

### Leukemia And Neuroblastoma

- Malignant infiltration of bone marrow, synovium, or bone causes severe musculoskeletal pain.
- Pain typically awakens child from sleep, indicating non-mechanical etiology.
- Deep pain elicited on palpation of long bones strongly suggests underlying malignancy.
- Fever pattern differs from typical quotidian fever characterizing systemic juvenile idiopathic arthritis.
- Peripheral blood smear appears normal during early disease stages.
- High erythrocyte sedimentation rate combined with leukopenia and low-normal platelet count strongly indicates leukemia.
- Diagnosis mandates comprehensive bone marrow examination.

## Infectious Etiologies

### Septic Arthritis And Osteomyelitis

- Acute onset of fever combined with painful, erythematous, hot joint suggests septic arthritis.
- Direct microbial invasion causes rapid, severe joint destruction.
- Clinical presentation includes refusal to bear weight and pseudoparalysis in infants.
- Synovial fluid aspiration reveals purulent exudate with extreme leukocytosis exceeding 50,000 cells per microliter.
- Management requires urgent intravenous antibiotics and possible surgical decompression.

### Viral And Post-Infectious Arthropathy

- Parvovirus B19, rubella, Epstein-Barr virus, and hepatitis B induce transient arthritis.
- Acute rheumatic fever causes severe, migratory polyarthritis following group A streptococcal infection.
- Transient synovitis of hip commonly follows viral upper respiratory infections, resolving spontaneously.

## Non-Inflammatory And Mechanical Conditions

### Benign Pain Syndromes

- Growing pains cause intermittent bilateral lower extremity aching occurring late in afternoon or evening.
- Pain completely resolves by morning with entirely normal physical examination.
- Joint hypermobility syndrome presents with pain exacerbation following physical activity.
- Management involves reassurance, physiotherapy, and symptomatic pain relief using simple analgesics.

### Orthopedic Abnormalities

- Slipped capital femoral epiphysis causes hip pain predominantly during adolescent growth spurts.
- Legg-Calve-Perthes disease presents with painful limp secondary to avascular necrosis of femoral head.
- Osgood-Schlatter disease involves osteochondrosis localized to tibial tuberosity.

## Congenital And Metabolic Disorders

### Mucopolysaccharidoses

- Glycosaminoglycan infiltration affects tendons, ligaments, and joint capsules.
- Condition features progressive joint contractures directly mimicking inflammatory arthritis.
- Joint stiffness does not exacerbate with rest or improve with physical activity.
- Distal interphalangeal joints frequently involved.
- Management requires metabolic evaluation and supportive care, lacking response to systemic corticosteroids.

### Progressive Pseudorheumatoid Dysplasia

- Autosomal recessive dysplasia involves primary articular cartilage.
- Joint stiffness occurs strictly without systemic inflammation.
- Erythrocyte sedimentation rate and C-reactive protein remain entirely normal.
- Imaging demonstrates characteristic dysplasia distinguishing condition from juvenile idiopathic arthritis.

## Hematologic And Immunodeficiency Disorders

### Hemophilic Arthropathy

- Recurrent hemarthrosis causes marked synovial proliferation.
- Heme iron deposition triggers progressive cartilage and subchondral bone destruction.
- Condition culminates in severe, crippling joint deformity.
- Prophylactic factor replacement prevents disease progression.

### Primary Immunodeficiencies

- Hypogammaglobulinemia and common variable immunodeficiency present with peripheral arthritis.
- Human immunodeficiency virus causes arthritis phenotypically resembling psoriatic arthritis.
