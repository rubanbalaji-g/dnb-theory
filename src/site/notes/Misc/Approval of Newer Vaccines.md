## Regulatory Framework And Approval Process

### Key Regulatory Authorities

- Central drugs standard control organization functions as national regulatory authority.
- Pharmacovigilance program of India and immunization division review safety data.
- India lacks vaccine-specific national regulatory authority for licensing, postmarketing surveillance, good manufacturing practices inspection, and clinical trial approval.
- National technical advisory group on immunization reviews disease burden, efficacy, and cost-effectiveness.
- Immunization technical support unit supports advisory group.
- Advisory group recommends new vaccine introduction to Ministry of health and family welfare.
- Adverse events following immunization surveillance system monitors safety signals after market introduction.

### Phases Of Vaccine Development

|Phase|Details|
|:--|:--|
|Preclinical|Identifies target antigen. Tests vaccine safety and efficacy in laboratory and animal models.|
|Phase one|Recruits dozens of participants. Tests vaccine dose level and safety.|
|Phase two|Recruits hundreds of participants. Evaluates immunogenicity and safety.|
|Phase three|Recruits thousands of participants. Establishes safety and efficacy.|
|Phase four|Postmarketing clinical trials. Gathers information regarding risks, benefits, and long-term effects. Tests product in special patient populations.|

## Recently Approved Newer Vaccines

### Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccines

- Drugs controller general of India approved ten-valent pneumococcal conjugate vaccine (Pneumosil) by Serum institute of India.
- Contains capsular polysaccharides of ten serotypes conjugated to cross-reactive material.
- Approved for active immunization against invasive disease and pneumonia in infants from six weeks.
- Subject expert committee of Central drugs standard control organization recommended fourteen-valent vaccine (Biological e) in August 2022.
- Demonstrates noninferiority to thirteen-valent vaccine.
- Twenty-valent pneumococcal vaccine licensed by Drugs controller general of India since June 2023 for adults over eighteen years.
- Thirteen-valent vaccine conjugated to tetanus toxoid (Pneumoguard) licensed for primary series at six, ten, and fourteen weeks.

### Typhoid Conjugate Vaccines

- Typbar-tcv licensed in India in 2013 for children aged six months and older.
- Demonstrates eighty-one percent efficacy at one year.
- Zyvac-tcv received marketing authorization in 2017 based on noninferiority trials.
- Typhibev licensed by Drugs controller general of India in February 2020.

### Human Papillomavirus Vaccines

- Quadrivalent human papillomavirus vaccine of Serum institute of India (Cervavac) granted market authorization.
- Vaccine developed by recombinant deoxyribonucleic acid technology producing noninfectious virus-like particles.
- Contains viral proteins of serotypes six, eleven, sixteen, and eighteen.

### Coronavirus Disease Vaccines

- Drugs controller general of India granted emergency use authorization to multiple vaccines for pediatric population.
- Covaxin approved for children aged six to twelve years as whole virion inactivated vaccine.
- Corbevax approved for children aged five to twelve years.
- Covovax approved as recombinant nanoparticle vaccine.
- Zycov-d granted emergency use authorization as plasmid deoxyribonucleic acid vaccine expressing spike protein.

### Rabies Vaccines

- Thrabis developed as novel three-dose recombinant nanoparticle-based rabies glycoprotein vaccine.
- Licensed by Drugs controller general of India for use above eighteen years of age.
- Monoclonal antibodies Twinrab and Rabishield available for postexposure prophylaxis.

### Meningococcal Vaccines

- Pentavalent meningococcal conjugate vaccine (Menfive) licensed in India in 2023 for individuals between eighteen and eighty-five years.
- Targets serogroups a, c, w, y, and x.
- Quadrivalent meningococcal conjugate vaccines Menactra and Menveo licensed earlier.
- Monovalent serogroup a conjugate vaccine licensed in India since 2009.

### Rotavirus Vaccines

- Rotavac developed by Bharat biotech as live naturally attenuated vaccine.
- Rotasiil developed as pentavalent virus vaccine.
- Both vaccines successfully completed phase three trials in India.
- Rolled out in national immunization program.

## Vaccines In Pipeline And Future Prospects

### Dengue Vaccines

- Dengvaxia not licensed in India due to requirement of additional safety data.
- Increased risk of severe dengue observed in seronegative individuals receiving Dengvaxia.
- Qdenga phase three trial data pending in India.
- Indian candidate vaccines include live attenuated and inactivated virus technologies.

### Malaria Vaccines

- World health organization approved Rts,s/as01 and R21/matrix-m vaccines globally.
- Indian introduction awaiting further evaluation in highly endemic tribal and hilly areas.
- Current focus remains on vector control and rapid detection until malarial vaccines roll out.

### Tuberculosis Vaccines

- Vpm1002 developed by Serum institute of India currently in phase three clinical trials.
- Recombinant live attenuated vaccine engineered to express listeriolysin.
- M72/as01e subunit vaccine candidate undergoing phase three trial.
- H4 vaccine combines Ic31 adjuvant with antigens.

### Shigella And Enterotoxigenic Escherichia Coli Vaccines

- Generalized modules for membrane antigen-based Shigella vaccine in early-stage clinical trials.
- Sf2a-tt15 conjugate vaccine targets Shigella flexneri.
- Etvax vaccine against enterotoxigenic Escherichia coli shows potential in phase two trials.

## Government Support For Indigenous Vaccine Industry

- National vaccine policy supports Indian vaccine industry.
- Department of biotechnology, Indian council of medical research, and Department of science and technology provide liberal support.
- Indian manufacturers supply approximately forty-three percent of global universal immunization program vaccines.
- Serum institute of India leads global production.
- Development of messenger ribonucleic acid, deoxyribonucleic acid, protein subunit, and vector-based vaccine platforms accelerated post-pandemic.
- Clinical trial networks established for rapid testing during health emergencies.
