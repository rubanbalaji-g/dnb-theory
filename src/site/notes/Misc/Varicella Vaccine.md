## General Characteristics

- Derived from live-attenuated Oka strain of varicella-zoster virus.
- Minimum 1000 plaque-forming units required per dose.
- Available as monovalent preparation or combined measles, mumps, rubella, and varicella formulations.
- Supplied as freeze-dried lyophilized powder requiring sterile diluent.
- Store vaccine and diluent strictly at 2-8°C.
- Protect from light and administer within 30 minutes of reconstitution.
- Administer 0.5 milliliter subcutaneously over anterolateral thigh or upper arm.

## Immunization Schedule

- Indian Academy Of Pediatrics recommends universal vaccination for children.
- Minimum administration age is 12 months.
- Preferred initiation at 15 months to minimize breakthrough infection risk.
- Two-dose primary schedule recommended.
- First dose administered at 15-18 months.
- Second dose administered 3-6 months post-first dose.
- Catch-up vaccination (<13 years): Two doses spaced 3 months apart.
- Catch-up vaccination (>13 years): Two doses spaced 4-8 weeks apart.
- Post-exposure prophylaxis: Administer within 3-5 days of exposure for optimal efficacy.

## Efficacy And Breakthrough Disease

- Breakthrough varicella occurs >42 days post-vaccination, typically 2-5 years later.
- Breakthrough clinical presentation remains mild, featuring <50 maculopapular lesions, minimal fever, and shorter illness duration.
- Two-dose schedules significantly reduce breakthrough incidence compared to single dose.

|Parameter|Single Dose|Two Doses|
|---|---|---|
|**Seroconversion Rate**|86 percent|99 percent|
|**Efficacy Against Mild Disease**|70-90 percent|98.3 percent|
|**Efficacy Against Moderate/Severe Disease**|>95 percent|100 percent|

## High-Risk Indications

- Chronic cardiac or pulmonary conditions.
- Asymptomatic HIV infection maintaining CD4 >15 percent.
- ALL in continuous remission >12 months, off chemotherapy, maintaining lymphocyte count >700/mm³ and platelet count >100,000/mm³.
- Children requiring prolonged salicylate therapy (salicylates must be withheld 6 weeks post-vaccination).
- Seronegative household contacts of immunocompromised individuals to prevent secondary transmission.

## Contraindications And Precautions

- Severe anaphylaxis history to previous dose or vaccine components including gelatin or neomycin.
- Pregnancy (must avoid conception 4 weeks post-vaccination).
- Severe cellular immunodeficiency or symptomatic human immunodeficiency virus infection.
- Recent administration of antibody-containing blood products or intravenous immunoglobulin (mandates 5-11 month delay).
- Concurrent moderate-to-severe acute illness or thrombocytopenia.
