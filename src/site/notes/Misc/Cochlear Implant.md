## Definition And Mechanism

- Prosthetic device designed to bypass injured organ of Corti.
- Directly stimulates residual cochlear nerve cells located within spiral ganglion.
- Converts environmental auditory stimuli into digital radiofrequency impulses.
- Replaces normal physiologic transmission involving hair cell sound vibration, ion release, and neurotransmitter activation.

## Device Components

- System combines externally worn and surgically implanted internal components.

|Component Category|Specific Parts|Functional Role|
|:--|:--|:--|
|External|Microphone|Detects environmental sounds.|
|External|Speech processor|Minicomputer digitizes and codes input signal using proprietary speech processing strategies.|
|External|Transmitter coil|Relays coded signals across skin via frequency modulation signal.|
|Internal|Receiver|Converts transmitted radiofrequency codes into electrical impulses.|
|Internal|Electrode array|Creates electrical fields acting directly on cochlear nerve.|

## Indications And Candidacy

- Indicated for bilateral severe to profound sensorineural hearing loss exceeding 70 decibels.
- Requires documented unsatisfactory benefit from conventional hearing aids after 3 to 6 month trial.
- Minimum age approved by United States Food and Drug Administration reduced to 9 months.
- Off-label utilization demonstrates efficacy in younger infants and children retaining residual hearing.
- Early intervention before 2 years of age optimizes outcomes, enabling over 90 percent of children to enter mainstream education.

## Preoperative Evaluation

- Requires comprehensive audiologic testing to establish baseline deficits.
- High-resolution computed tomography assesses bony temporal bone and inner ear anatomy.
- Magnetic resonance imaging confirms presence and structural integrity of cochlear nerve.

## Surgical Management

- Procedure performed under general anesthesia.
- Involves mastoidectomy coupled with widening of facial recess to access inner ear.
- Internal stimulator package fastened securely within mastoid process.
- Cochlea opened typically via round window to insert multielectrode array into scala tympani.
- Extreme caution exercised to prevent cochlear fluid contamination by bone dust or blood.
- Intraoperative audiologist testing immediately verifies device functional integrity via cranial nerve VIII electrophysiologic responses.
- Intraoperative plain radiograph confirms precise electrode array placement.

## Postoperative Rehabilitation

- Initial healing period spans 3 to 4 weeks, during which child remains without auditory input.
- Initial stimulation establishes baseline programming for first sound access.
- Program modification relies on behavioral testing and observed vocalization changes.
- Demands intensive, long-term commitment to speech and language therapy.
- Bilateral implantation preferred over unilateral procedure to improve sound localization and speech understanding in noisy environments.

## Complications And Special Considerations

|Complication/Issue|Clinical Management And Prevention|
|:--|:--|
|Pneumococcal meningitis|Represents severe potential complication. Strict mandate requires pneumococcal polyvalent vaccine (PCV13) administration prior to surgery.|
|Acute mastoiditis|Occurs in 3.5 percent of implanted children. Successfully managed with intravenous antibiotics, tympanostomy tubes, and abscess drainage without requiring implant removal.|
|Magnetic resonance imaging incompatibility|Strong magnetic fields risk implant damage. Most modern implants verified safe only for scanners up to 3.0 Tesla.|
