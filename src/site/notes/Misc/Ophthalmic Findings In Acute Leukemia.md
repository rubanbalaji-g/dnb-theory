## Anterior Segment Manifestations

- Iris infiltration occurs frequently.
- Hypopyon develops secondary to white blood cell accumulation within anterior chamber.
- Anterior segment involvement frequently heralds disease relapse.
- Represents early indicator of central nervous system involvement.

## Posterior Segment Manifestations

### Leukemic Retinopathy

- Veins appear characteristically dilated.
- Venous structure demonstrates sausage-shaped constrictions.
- Retinal hemorrhages manifest frequently during acute stages.
- White-centered hemorrhages (Roth spots) represent classic fundoscopic finding.
- Retinal exudates accompany hemorrhagic changes.
- Leukemic ophthalmopathy potentially presents as leukocoria (white pupillary reflex).

## Orbital And Neuro-Ophthalmic Manifestations

### Orbital Involvement

- Orbital tumors result from metastatic leukemic spread.
- Malignant infiltration mimics orbital cellulitis.
- Dacryoadenitis develops secondary to lacrimal gland infiltration.

### Neuro-Ophthalmic Signs

- Visual loss indicates central nervous system involvement.
- Condition potentially manifests as uveo-meningeal syndrome.

## Summary Of Findings

|Anatomic Region|Specific Clinical Finding|Clinical Significance|
|:--|:--|:--|
|Anterior Chamber|Hypopyon and iris infiltration|Heralds relapse or central nervous system involvement.|
|Retina|Sausage-shaped dilated veins|Indicates leukemic retinopathy during acute stage.|
|Retina|White-centered hemorrhages (Roth spots)|Classic posterior segment manifestation.|
|Orbit|Extraocular mass or proptosis|Suggests metastatic spread mimicking cellulitis.|
|Lacrimal Gland|Dacryoadenitis|Represents localized glandular leukemic infiltration.|
|Central Nervous System|Profound visual loss|Denotes leukemic extension into neuro-ophthalmic pathways.|
