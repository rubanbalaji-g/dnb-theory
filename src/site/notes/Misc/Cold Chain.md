## Definition

- System transporting and storing vaccines within recommended temperature range.
- Extends from place of manufacture to point of administration.
- Ensures vaccine potency and safety.

## Key Components

- Personnel: Trained staff managing vaccine storage.
- Equipment: Storage and temperature monitoring devices.
- Procedures: Protocols for storage, handling, and emergency retrieval.

## Vaccine Sensitivities And Storage Temperatures

- Optimum temperature for refrigerated vaccines is +2°C to +8°C.
- Optimum temperature for frozen vaccines is -15°C to -25°C.
- Cold-sensitive vaccines experience immediate loss of potency following freezing.
- Repetitive exposure to heat causes cumulative, irreversible loss of potency.

### Vaccine Storage Matrix

|Vaccine Type|Heat/Light Sensitivity|Freeze Sensitivity|Storage Temperature|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|BCG (freeze-dried)|Sensitive to light|Not damaged by freezing|+2°C to +8°C|
|OPV|Heat sensitive|Not damaged by freezing|+2°C to +8°C|
|Measles, MR, MMR|Sensitive to heat and light|Not damaged by freezing|+2°C to +8°C|
|DPT, DT, Td, Hep B|Relatively heat-stable|Damaged by freezing|+2°C to +8°C|

## Cold Chain Equipment

### Electrical Equipment

- Walk-in freezers: Bulk storage of OPV and ice packs at -18°C to -20°C.
- Walk-in coolers: Bulk storage at state/regional stores at +2°C to +8°C.
- Deep freezers: Top-opening lids storing OPV and freezing ice packs.
- Ice-lined refrigerators: Line of water containers prevents rapid warming during power failures.

### Non-Electrical Equipment

- Cold boxes: Insulated boxes with ice packs for transport.
- Vaccine carriers: Transporting 16-20 vials for field use.
- Ice packs: Leak-proof plastic containers frozen at -25°C.

### Implementation Hierarchy

|Center Level|Storage Equipment|Temperature|Duration|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|Manufacturer/National Store|Walk-in cooler/freezer|-25°C to -15°C / +2°C to +8°C|≥3 months|
|District Vaccine Store|Deep freezer, Ice-lined refrigerator|-25°C to -15°C / +2°C to +8°C|1-3 months|
|Primary Health Center|Ice-lined refrigerator, Cold boxes|+2°C to +8°C|1-3 months|

## Ice-Lined Refrigerator Storage Arrangement

- Bottom compartment is coldest part.
- Store heat-sensitive vaccines at bottom: Measles, MR, MMR, BCG, OPV, Yellow fever, live JE, Varicella, Rotavirus.
- Middle and upper compartments store freeze-sensitive vaccines.
- Store heat-resistant vaccines at top: Pertussis-containing combination vaccines, inactivated hepatitis A, TCV, PCV, HPV, Rabies.

## Temperature Monitoring Tools

- Minimum/maximum thermometers: Record temperature fluctuations.
- Data loggers: Build temperature map electronically.
- Vaccine vial monitors: Heat-sensitive labels showing cumulative heat exposure.
- Color change directly relates to rate of heat exposure.
- Electronic freeze indicators: Display alarm if exposed below 0°C for 60 minutes.
- Shake test: Detects if adsorbed vaccine erroneously frozen.

## Electronic Vaccine Intelligence Network

- IT-based system strengthening vaccine supply chain.
- Enables real-time monitoring of vaccine stocks and storage temperatures.
- Digital sensors record temperature every 10 minutes.
- Generates alarms and alerts for temperature breaches.
