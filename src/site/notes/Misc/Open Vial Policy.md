## Policy Overview

- Represents World Health Organization Multi-Dose Vaccine Policy.
- Governs safe usage and storage duration of opened multidose vaccine vials.
- Aims to minimize vaccine wastage while ensuring strict immunization safety.

## Usage Protocols Based On Vaccine Vial Monitor

### Standard 28-Day Protocol

- Applies to liquid or freeze-dried multidose vials possessing Vaccine Vial Monitor attached directly over vaccine label.
- Permits vaccine utilization for next 28 days following initial vial opening.
- Requires maintaining proper cold chain storage during entire period.

### Restricted 6-Hour Protocol

- Applies to vials possessing Vaccine Vial Monitor attached on cap or neck of ampoule.
- Mandates discarding opened vial immediately after immunization session concludes.
- Requires disposal within maximum 6 hours of opening, whichever occurs first.

## Reconstituted Vaccines Timelines

- Lyophilized vaccines dictate specific maximum time allowances between reconstitution and administration.

|Time Limit|Specific Vaccines|
|:--|:--|
|30 Minutes|Diphtheria-Tetanus-acellular Pertussis-Inactivated Polio-Hemophilus influenzae type b, Measles-Mumps-Rubella-Varicella, Rabies, Varicella, Zoster, Hemophilus influenzae type b with Meningococcal C/Y.|
|4 Hours|Bacillus Calmette-Guérin.|
|6 Hours|Measles-Rubella vaccine.|
|8 Hours|Measles-Mumps-Rubella, Meningococcal.|
|24 Hours|Hemophilus influenzae type b, Rotavirus.|

## Quality And Safety Indicators

### Physical Appearance Checks

- Discard reconstituted vaccines exhibiting abnormal discoloration.
- Avoid usage if visible particulate matter appears.
- Discard formulation upon inability to properly suspend lyophilized powder.

### Vaccine Vial Monitor Checks

- Requires continuous monitoring of heat-sensitive square within outer circle.
- Vaccine remains usable as long as inner square remains lighter than outer circle.
- Mandates immediate discard if inner square color matches or becomes darker than outer circle.
