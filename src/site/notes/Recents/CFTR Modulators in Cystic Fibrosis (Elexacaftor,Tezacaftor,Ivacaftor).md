## Introduction and Mechanism of Action

- Elexacaftor/tezacaftor/ivacaftor (ETI) constitutes a highly effective CFTR modulator triple therapy (HEMT).
- It shifts Cystic Fibrosis (CF) from a progressive fatal disease to a manageable chronic condition by directly addressing the underlying misfolded protein defect.

|Modulator|Pharmacological Role|
|:--|:--|
|**Ivacaftor**|Potentiator: Increases the open probability of the CFTR channel at the cell surface.|
|**Tezacaftor**|Corrector: Facilitates cellular processing and trafficking of the CFTR protein to the cell membrane.|
|**Elexacaftor**|Stabilizer: Further corrects and stabilizes the misfolded CFTR protein.|

## Eligibility and Screening Guidelines

- **Approval Criteria:** Approved by the FDA and EMA for children $\ge$ 2 years possessing at least one F508del mutation or responsive variants.
- **Coverage:** Recent 2026 label expansions cover 90-95% of CF patients globally.
- **Initiation:** Initiate early at the youngest eligible age without any lung function threshold to maximize benefit.
- **Monitoring Protocol (CFF 2025):** Mandates quarterly surveillance of liver function tests, ophthalmology (for cataracts), and growth parameters.
- **Drug Interactions:** Requires careful monitoring and shared decision-making regarding CYP3A inducers and inhibitors.

## Clinical Efficacy and Outcomes (2024-2026 Data)

- Early preschool intervention maximizes benefits by preventing irreversible lung damage and averting frequent hospitalizations.

|Clinical Domain|Demonstrated Outcomes (PROMISE & Real-World Studies)|
|:--|:--|
|**Pulmonary Function**|Improves ppFEV1 by 10-14.2%; reduces acute pulmonary exacerbations by 60-65%.|
|**Microbiology**|Rapid reduction in _Pseudomonas_ and _Aspergillus_ colonization (61% drop in _Aspergillus_ by Year 2).|
|**Gastrointestinal & Nutritional**|Rapid BMI improvement (z-score normalization); improved exocrine pancreatic function and nutrient absorption.|
|**Biochemical Markers**|Reduces sweat chloride significantly (average reduction of 50-60 mmol/L).|
|**Therapy De-escalation**|Reduces mean chronic supportive therapies (inhaled antibiotics, hypertonic saline, dornase alfa, azithromycin) from 2.6 to 1.4 per patient.|

## Safety Profile

- Generally well-tolerated with mild adverse effects like headache and rash occurring in <10% of patients.
- Indian pharmacogenetic studies confirm that CYP3A5 variants have minimal impact on ETI response.

## The Indian Context and Implementation Challenges

- **Epidemiology:** F508del mutation prevalence in India is 25-40%, which is significantly lower than Western populations (70-90%), requiring responsive variant testing for eligibility.
- **Diagnostic Gaps:** Underestimated true incidence (likely 1:10,000–1:40,000 births) limits identification due to delayed diagnosis and minimal neonatal screening.
- **Access Barriers:** Extremely high cost (₹2-3 crore/year) and prolonged patent barriers up to 2037 restrict equitable access.
- **Policy Integration:** Supported under the National Policy for Rare Diseases (NPRD) Group 3, offering financial support up to ₹20 lakh/patient via specialized Centres of Excellence.
- **Pediatrician's Role:** Suspect CF early, confirm via sweat chloride and Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) genotyping panels, and aggressively advocate for biosimilars or compassionate access programs.
