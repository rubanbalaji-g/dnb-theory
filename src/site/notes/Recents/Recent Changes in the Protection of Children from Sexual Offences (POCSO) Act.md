## Introduction And Legislative Context

- The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012, remains the cornerstone legislation for safeguarding children under 18 years from sexual abuse.
- While no major legislative amendment to the core Act occurred between 2024 and 2026 (the last being the 2019 Amendment introducing the death penalty for aggravated assault), significant procedural and judicial updates have emerged.
- These updates aim to balance stringent protection with fairness in adolescent cases, strengthen evidence collection, and reduce secondary trauma.

## Procedural Reforms Under BNSS 2023

- The Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023, effective July 1, 2024, introduces major procedural rationalizations.
- **Forensic Mandates:** It mandates forensic investigation for serious POCSO offenses punishable with 7 or more years of imprisonment.
- **Electronic Proceedings:** It allows electronic trials and proceedings to facilitate child-friendly courts, which has reduced trial delays by 30% to 40%.

## Supreme Court Directives (2024–2026)

|Judicial Area|Supreme Court Directive / Observation|Clinical Implication|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**"Romeo-Juliet" Cases**|Raised concerns over the misuse of the Act in consensual adolescent relationships (close-in-age, 16–18 years).|Pediatricians must document relationship history sensitively while complying with mandatory reporting rules.|
|**Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)**|Clarified that mere possession of CSAM is a criminal offense, with intent inferred from failure to delete or report.|Strengthens digital evidence handling and reporting mandates.|
|**Age Determination**|Rejected mandatory age tests at the bail stage in POCSO cases, limiting them to prima facie documents.|Reduces unnecessary medical interventions and victim trauma during early legal proceedings.|

## MoHFW And IAP Guidelines (2025 Updates)

- The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) and the Indian Academy of Pediatrics (IAP) have standardized medical handling protocols to ensure gender-sensitive, child-friendly examinations.

|Protocol Domain|2025 Guideline Mandates|
|:--|:--|
|**Consent**|Medical examination must proceed with informed consent from the child (if older than 12 years) or the guardian.|
|**Examination Personnel**|Strictly mandates a female doctor for examining female victims.|
|**Support System**|Requires the presence of a guardian or a trusted adult during the examination.|
|**Invasive Testing**|Strongly advises avoiding unnecessary invasive tests (e.g., in groping cases) to prevent mental agony and secondary trauma.|

## Pediatrician's Medico-Legal Responsibilities

- **Mandatory Reporting:** Pediatricians act as mandatory reporters under Section 19 of the POCSO Act; failure to report attracts legal punishment.
- **Medical Examination:** Must be conducted per Section 27 guidelines, ensuring evidence preservation and chain of custody.
- **Documentation:** Clinical findings must be recorded factually without speculation.
- **Legal Protection:** High Court rulings have reinforced that medical examinations conducted with proper parental consent are legal and protect doctors from criminal liability under POCSO, alleviating medico-legal fears.
