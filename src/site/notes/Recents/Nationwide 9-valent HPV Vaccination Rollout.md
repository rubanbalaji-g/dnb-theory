## Epidemiological Context

- Human Papillomavirus (HPV) is the primary etiological driver of cervical, vulvar, vaginal, anal, penile, and oropharyngeal malignancies, alongside anogenital warts.
- India accounts for approximately 25% of global cervical cancer deaths, with over 1,20,000 new cases and nearly 80,000 deaths annually.
- The nationwide immunization campaign, launched in February 2026, aims to shift cervical cancer from an oncology burden to a preventable infectious disease, aligning with the World Health Organization (WHO) 90-70-90 elimination threshold.

## Antigenic Profile and Efficacy

- HPV vaccines contain virus-like particles (VLPs) of the major capsid L1 protein produced via recombinant DNA technology.
- **Quadrivalent Vaccine:** Targets low-risk strains 6 and 11, and high-risk oncogenic strains 16 and 18.
- **9-Valent Vaccine (9vHPV):** Expands protection against five additional high-risk oncogenic strains (31, 33, 45, 52, and 58).
- 9vHPV escalates cervical cancer protection from 70-83% to 90-98%, demonstrating near-complete prevention of persistent infections and high-grade cervical lesions (CIN2/3, AIS) in long-term follow-up studies.

## Clinical Guidelines and Dosing Schedules

|Parameter|National Immunization Program (Feb 2026)|IAP ACVIP 2025 Recommendations|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Target Demographic**|14-year-old girls.|Gender-neutral (boys and girls).|
|**Preferred Vaccine**|Quadrivalent HPV vaccine.|9-valent (9vHPV) preferred for broader coverage.|
|**Primary Schedule (9-14 years)**|Single dose administered at government facilities.|2-dose schedule (0 and 6-12 months).|
|**Catch-up Schedule (15-26 years)**|Routine rollout focused on early adolescents.|3-dose schedule (0, 2, and 6 months).|
|**Immunocompromised Patients**|Standard public health triage.|3-dose schedule (0, 2, and 6 months) regardless of age at initiation.|

## Administration and Logistics

- **Delivery:** Administered as a 0.5 mL intramuscular injection in the deltoid muscle.
- **Cold Chain Maintenance:** Requires continuous storage between 2°C and 8°C. Freezing is strictly contraindicated as it causes irreversible denaturation of the aluminum adjuvant, destroying vaccine potency.
- **Post-Vaccination Protocol:** Rollout protocols mandate a compulsory 15-minute post-injection observation period with the recipient seated or supine to prevent secondary injuries from vasovagal syncope.

## Implementation Barriers and Mitigations

- **Socio-Cultural Stigma:** Vaccine hesitancy linked to fears regarding early sexual activity or long-term fertility is mitigated by re-framing the communication strictly as an anti-cancer vaccine rather than an STI vaccine.
- **Economic Constraints:** The high procurement cost of 9vHPV is a barrier; public health strategies rely on tiered purchasing, international Gavi partnerships, and indigenous quadrivalent production (e.g., Cervavac) to establish widespread baseline coverage.
- **Surveillance:** Adverse Events Following Immunization (AEFI) are actively monitored, and electronic registries like U-WIN track stock levels and trace multi-dose defaulters.
