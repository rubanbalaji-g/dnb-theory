## Paradigm Shift To Phoenix Criteria

- The Surviving Sepsis Campaign (SSC) 2026 fully adopts the 2024 Phoenix Pediatric Sepsis Criteria, completely abandoning the outdated Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS) definitions.
- Sepsis is strictly defined as suspected or proven infection accompanied by a Phoenix Sepsis Score of 2 points or higher.
- Pediatric septic shock is defined as meeting sepsis criteria alongside cardiovascular dysfunction, requiring at least 1 point in the cardiovascular domain of the Phoenix score.

## The Resuscitation Bundle And Hemodynamic Management

- The 1-hour resuscitation bundle emphasizes individualized, phenotype-driven hemodynamics and early vasoactive initiation.

|Parameter|2026 SSC Recommendation|Rationale And Clinical Implementation|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Fluid Resuscitation**|10 to 20 mL/kg aliquots over 15 to 30 minutes|Replaces historical aggressive 60 mL/kg boluses.|
|**Fluid Cap**|Maximum 40 mL/kg without advanced monitoring|Prevents fluid overload, which independently drives mortality via pulmonary edema and abdominal compartment syndrome.|
|**Fluid Choice**|Balanced crystalloids (Plasmalyte, Ringer's Lactate)|Strongly preferred over 0.9% normal saline to prevent hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis and acute kidney injury.|
|**Vasoactive Initiation**|Early and peripheral administration|Initiate via peripheral intravenous or intraosseous line if shock persists after 20-40 mL/kg fluid; do not wait for central access.|
|**Drug Selection**|Epinephrine for cold shock; Norepinephrine for warm shock|Drug titration must be strictly guided by multimodal monitoring to match specific hemodynamic phenotypes.|
|**Monitoring**|Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS)|Dynamically assess left ventricular contractility, right ventricular strain, and fluid responsiveness before and after small fluid challenges.|

## Antimicrobial Stewardship And Source Control

- Intravenous antimicrobials must be administered within 1 hour of recognition for pediatric septic shock.
- For sepsis without shock, a 3-hour window is permissible to perform rapid diagnostic evaluations, utilizing rapid syndromic multiplex PCR panels to identify specific pathogens before administering broad-spectrum antibiotics.
- Blood cultures must be drawn prior to antibiotic administration, provided it does not delay the 1-hour window.
- Source control for undrained abscesses, infected central lines, or necrotic tissue must be achieved within 6 to 12 hours.

## Adjunctive Therapies

- Routine stress-dose steroids are abandoned; intravenous hydrocortisone is strictly reserved for fluid-refractory, catecholamine-resistant septic shock or suspected absolute adrenal insufficiency.
- Metabolic resuscitation cocktails combining Vitamin C, thiamine, and hydrocortisone demonstrate zero mortality benefit, increase oxalate nephropathy risk, and are strongly recommended against.
- Hemoadsorption cartridges (e.g., CytoSorb) integrated into continuous renal replacement therapy are utilized to aggressively filter cytokine storms in hyperinflammatory phenotypes.
- Veno-arterial extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (VA-ECMO) remains the ultimate salvage therapy for refractory pediatric septic shock.

## Artificial Intelligence And Post-Intensive Care

- Targeted Real-time Early Warning Systems (TREWS) utilizing machine learning algorithms predict septic shock up to 12 hours before overt clinical deterioration.
- Post-Intensive Care Syndrome in pediatrics (PICS-p) affects up to 30% of sepsis survivors, manifesting as long-term cognitive impairments, acquired neuromuscular weakness, and profound psychological trauma.
- Structured, multi-disciplinary follow-up clinics are mandated post-discharge to manage PICS-p.

## Specific Considerations For Tropical Settings

- In suspected scrub typhus, early empirical doxycycline or azithromycin is mandated alongside the sepsis bundle.
- Fluid resuscitation in dengue shock syndrome must be managed with extreme caution due to profound endothelial capillary leak to prevent lethal abdominal compartment syndrome.
- High rates of carbapenem-resistant Enterobacteriaceae in Indian intensive care units require empirical first-line escalation to novel beta-lactam/beta-lactamase inhibitors or colistin strictly guided by local antibiograms.
