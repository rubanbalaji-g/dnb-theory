## Introduction and Rationale

- BPaLM is a 6-month (24-week), entirely oral, four-drug combination regimen utilized for Multidrug-Resistant Tuberculosis (MDR-TB) and Rifampicin-Resistant TB (RR-TB).
- Replaces historically prolonged 18 to 24-month regimens that mandated toxic, daily injectable aminoglycosides causing irreversible sensorineural hearing loss.
- Highly effective, achieving an 89% clinical success rate in the TB-PRACTECAL phase 3 trial compared to a 52% success rate with older standards of care.

## Pharmacological Composition and Mechanisms

|Drug|Mechanism of Action|Key Characteristics|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Bedaquiline (Bdq)**|Targets mycobacterial ATP synthase, effectively starving the bacteria of cellular energy.|First-in-class diarylquinoline; highly effective against replicating and dormant bacilli.|
|**Pretomanid (Pa)**|Inhibits mycolic acid biosynthesis under aerobic conditions and generates reactive nitrogen species under anaerobic conditions.|Novel nitroimidazooxazine antimicrobial.|
|**Linezolid (Lzd)**|Inhibits mycobacterial protein synthesis by binding to 23S ribosomal RNA.|Requires close vigilance due to time- and dose-dependent mitochondrial toxicities.|
|**Moxifloxacin (Mfx)**|Inhibits DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV enzymes, arresting DNA replication.|Dropped from the regimen if fluoroquinolone resistance is present, leaving the BPaL regimen.|

## Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

- **Inclusion:** The WHO and NTEP guidelines strongly recommend BPaLM as the primary treatment of choice, explicitly extending this to adolescents aged 14 years and older.
- **Age Restriction:** Currently not approved for children under 14 years; trials like IMPAACT 2034 and BEAT-India are actively investigating Pretomanid pharmacokinetics for younger pediatric cohorts.
- **Exclusion Criteria:**
    - Severe forms of extrapulmonary TB, specifically TB meningitis, osteoarticular TB, or disseminated/miliary TB with multiorgan involvement.
    - Pregnant or breastfeeding adolescents.
    - Patients with prior exposure exceeding one month to Bedaquiline, Linezolid, or Pretomanid/Delamanid without explicit drug susceptibility testing confirming ongoing sensitivity.

## Dosing Schedule (Adolescents $\ge$ 14 Years)

- All medications are administered orally under Directly Observed Treatment (DOT) for exactly 26 weeks (6 months).
- **Bedaquiline:** Loading dose of 400 mg once daily for the first 14 days, followed by a maintenance dose of 200 mg three times per week.
- **Pretomanid:** 200 mg once daily.
- **Linezolid:** Initiated at 600 mg once daily. Dose is reduced to 300 mg daily if severe myelosuppression or neuropathy develops.
- **Moxifloxacin:** 400 mg once daily.
- **Adjunctive Therapy:** Mandatory co-administration of Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6) at 50-100 mg daily for the entire duration to proactively mitigate Linezolid-induced peripheral neuropathy.

## Monitoring Protocols

|Monitoring Domain|Protocol and Intervention|
|:--|:--|
|**Cardiovascular**|Baseline ECG is mandatory. Both Bedaquiline and Moxifloxacin prolong the QT interval. Withhold drugs and correct electrolytes if QTcF exceeds 500 ms.|
|**Hematological and Neurological**|Baseline and monthly Complete Blood Count (CBC) required. Clinical screening for peripheral/optic neuropathy at every visit. Reduce Linezolid dose if toxicity manifests.|
|**Hepatic**|Baseline and monthly Liver Function Tests (LFTs). Interrupt therapy if transaminases rise above 5 times the upper limit of normal.|
|**Microbiological**|Sputum smear microscopy and liquid mycobacterial culture performed monthly from month 3 onwards. Cure declared after full 6 months completion with at least three consecutive negative cultures.|
