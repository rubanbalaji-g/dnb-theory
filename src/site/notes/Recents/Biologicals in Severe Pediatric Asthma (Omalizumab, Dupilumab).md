## Introduction and Clinical Burden

- Target Type 2 inflammation in severe pediatric asthma refractory to optimized Step 4-5 therapy (high-dose ICS-LABA) or oral corticosteroid (OCS) dependence.
- Approved for pediatric patients $\ge$ 6 years of age.
- Mitigate poor disease control, which otherwise causes school absenteeism, irreversible airway remodeling, and growth impairment.

## Mechanisms and Biomarker-Driven Selection

### Omalizumab (Anti-IgE)

- Mechanism: Binds free IgE, directly reducing mast cell activation.
- Phenotype: Indicated for allergic asthma demonstrating perennial aeroallergen sensitization.
- Biomarker Requirement: Total serum IgE levels between 30-1500 IU/mL.
- Dosing: Administered via subcutaneous injection every 2-4 weeks, calculated based on body weight and baseline IgE levels.
- Durability: Benefits may persist up to 1 year post-discontinuation in carefully selected cases.

### Dupilumab (Anti-IL-4R$\alpha$)

- Mechanism: Blocks IL-4 and IL-13 signaling pathways, addressing broader inflammatory cascades.
- Phenotype: Eosinophilic, allergic, or OCS-dependent Type 2 inflammation.
- Biomarker Requirement: Blood eosinophil count (BEC) $\ge$ 150-300/$\mu$L or Fractional Exhaled Nitric Oxide (FeNO) $\ge$ 20-25 ppb.
- Dosing: Subcutaneous injection 200/300 mg every 2 weeks.

## Latest Evidence and Research (2025-2026)

- Efficacy: Biologics collectively reduce severe exacerbations by 50-65%, enhance FEV1, and minimize OCS exposure.
- VOYAGE Trial (Dupilumab): Achieved 59-65% exacerbation reduction and improved ACQ-7 scores in children aged 6-11 years, with 77-79% remaining exacerbation-free at 52 weeks.
- Comparative Data: Dupilumab demonstrates superiority over omalizumab in patients with frequent exacerbations, FEV1 <70% predicted, or comorbid nasal polyps.
- Timing: Early biologic initiation ($\le$ 11 years) correlates with 53% lower exacerbation odds, amplifying benefits in polysensitized children.
- Safety: Mild adverse events include injection-site reactions, eosinophilia, and viral upper respiratory infections. Serious adverse events remain <2%.

## Society Guidelines and Protocols

### GINA 2025/2026 Updates

- Positioned definitively as Step 5 add-on therapy.
- Decision Tree: Dupilumab preferred for eosinophilic/OCS-dependent phenotypes; Omalizumab preferred for strictly IgE-mediated allergy.
- Emphasizes biomarker reassessment (BEC/FeNO $\ge$ 3 times) if baseline values are borderline.

### CHEST 2026 and IAP Alignment

- CHEST 2026 favors dupilumab for patients with $\ge$ 2 exacerbations annually, prior hospitalizations, or steroid dependence.
- IAP 2025-2026 strongly endorses GINA-aligned, phenotype-driven selection following early referral to a pediatric pulmonologist or allergist.

## Implementation Challenges in India

- Economic Barriers: High procurement costs (₹20,000-50,000 per dose) restrict widespread access.
- Logistics: Cold-chain maintenance, limited specialist availability, and injection anxiety hinder compliance.
- Government Initiatives: MoHFW and ICMR Standard Treatment Workflows (STWs) acknowledge biologics for refractory cases, facilitating limited access via CGHS, Railways, and select Ayushman Bharat packages.
- Future Directions: Integration of biosimilars (e.g., omalizumab-igec) is urgently needed to drive cost-reduction and achieve treatment equity.
