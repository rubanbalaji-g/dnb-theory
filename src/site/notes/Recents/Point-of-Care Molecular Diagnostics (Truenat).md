## Introduction and Principle

- Truenat is an indigenously developed, chip-based, battery-operated, real-time micro-PCR platform designed to decentralize molecular testing.
- Developed by Molbio Diagnostics, it utilizes Taqman real-time quantitative reverse transcription-polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) or standard DNA PCR miniaturized onto disposable microchips.
- Reagents and chips are freeze-dried and stable at room temperature (2°C to 30°C), completely eliminating the need for complex cold-chain logistics.

## Hardware and Workflow

|Component / Step|Operational Characteristics|
|:--|:--|
|**Trueprep AUTO V2**|Fully automated sample preparation device executing nucleic acid extraction and purification.|
|**Truelab Analyzer**|Performs rapid thermal cycling and fluorescent detection; available in single (Uno Dx), dual (Duo), or quad (Quattro) testing bays.|
|**1. Collection & Lysis**|Biological specimen is dispensed into a proprietary lysis buffer, neutralizing the infectious agent immediately (5 minutes).|
|**2. Extraction**|Trueprep AUTO automatically purifies high-quality nucleic acids, removing amplification inhibitors (20 minutes).|
|**3. Amplification**|Microchip is inserted into the Truelab analyzer for real-time quantification (35 minutes).|

## Advantages in Pediatric Practice

- **High Sensitivity:** Detects minute quantities of pathogen DNA or RNA, which is essential for diagnosing paucibacillary pediatric infections and preventing false negatives.
- **Rapid Turnaround:** Delivers results within 60 minutes, enabling a definitive "Test and Treat" strategy during a single outpatient visit.
- **Minimal Sample Volume:** Requires only microscopic aliquots of extracted nucleic acids, making it highly suitable for neonates and young infants.
- **Antimicrobial Stewardship (AMS):** Rapid pathogen identification allows pediatricians to execute targeted use of Access, Watch, and Reserve (AWaRe) antibiotics.

## Major Clinical Applications

- **Tuberculosis (TB):** Utilizes Truenat MTB and MTB Plus assays for initial detection. Positive samples undergo reflex testing with the Truenat MTB-RIF Dx assay to detect rifampicin resistance within an additional hour. Highly effective for challenging pediatric samples like gastric aspirates, induced sputum, stool, and cerebrospinal fluid.
- **Respiratory Pathogens:** Offers rapid diagnostic assays for SARS-CoV-2, Influenza A and B, and Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV).
- **Vector-Borne Diseases:** Facilitates diagnosis of Acute Undifferentiated Febrile Illness (AUFI) etiologies, including Dengue, [[Infectious Diseases/Malaria\|Malaria]], Chikungunya, Scrub Typhus, and Leptospirosis.

## National Guidelines and Health Economics

- **National Tuberculosis Elimination Programme (NTEP):** Mandates upfront molecular testing using Truenat or CBNAAT as the first-line diagnostic investigation for all children presenting with presumptive pulmonary or extrapulmonary TB.
- **Global Endorsements:** The World Health Organization (WHO) explicitly endorses Truenat MTB, MTB Plus, and MTB-RIF Dx as initial diagnostic tests replacing traditional sputum smear microscopy.
- **Cost-Effectiveness:** Implementing Truenat as a primary point-of-care test significantly improves linkage-to-care metrics, proving highly cost-effective with an Incremental Cost-Effectiveness Ratio (ICER) of $210 per Year of Life Saved (YLS).
