## Introduction And Pharmacology

- Nirsevimab is a recombinant human IgG1-kappa monoclonal antibody engineered for the prevention of Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) lower respiratory tract disease in infants.
- It incorporates YTE technology, representing a triple amino acid substitution in the Fc region to significantly increase binding affinity to the neonatal Fc receptor.
- This structural modification delays clearance and extends the serum half-life to approximately 60 to 100 days.
- A single intramuscular dose delivers sustained passive immunization for at least 5 months, providing continuous protection throughout an entire RSV season.

## Mechanism Of Action

- Nirsevimab binds with high affinity to Antigenic Site Zero on the prefusion conformation of the RSV fusion (F) protein.
- By locking the viral F protein in its prefusion state, it physically blocks the conformational transition required for viral attachment and membrane fusion with host respiratory epithelial cells, thereby preventing viral entry and replication.

## Clinical Indications And Guidelines

- The World Health Organization 2025 guidelines recommend nirsevimab for all infants to protect against severe RSV.
- The Indian Academy of Pediatrics Advisory Committee on Vaccines and Immunization Practices (ACVIP) 2025 schedule recommends it primarily for high-risk infants following parental consultation.
- High-risk conditions mandating prophylaxis include prematurity under 35 weeks, chronic lung disease, hemodynamically significant congenital heart disease, neuromuscular disorders, and severely immunocompromised states.
- The National Neonatology Forum 2025 Position Statement prioritizes high-risk babies in public settings but advises informing parents of healthy newborns for private use.
- It is indicated universally for infants under 8 months entering their first RSV season and for high-risk pediatric patients aged 8 to 19 months entering their second RSV season.

## Dosage And Administration Protocol

- Nirsevimab is administered as a single intramuscular injection into the anterolateral aspect of the thigh.

|Patient Category|Weight And Age Criteria|Recommended Dose|
|---|---|---|
|First RSV Season|Less than 5 kg|50 mg|
|First RSV Season|5 kg or more|100 mg|
|Second RSV Season|High-risk, 8 to 19 months|200 mg (administered as two separate 100 mg injections into distinct anatomical sites)|

## Efficacy And Safety Profile

- Real-world data from the 2025-2026 seasons confirm 70% to 89% effectiveness against RSV-associated hospitalizations and an 80% effectiveness against intensive care unit admissions.
- It offers a major operational advantage over traditional palivizumab prophylaxis, which required strictly timed, monthly intramuscular injections and was restricted to highly select preterm cohorts.
- Nirsevimab provides immediate passive immunity without interfering with the active immune response generated by routine childhood vaccines.
- The safety profile remains excellent, with the most frequently reported adverse events being mild, self-limiting injection site reactions and transient pyrexia.
- Severe hypersensitivity or anaphylaxis to nirsevimab or its constituent excipients constitutes an absolute contraindication.

## Disease Burden And Indian Context

- RSV remains a leading cause of hospitalization in children under 5 years, causing an estimated 3 to 5 million annual episodes in India.
- The Central Drugs Standard Control Organization granted marketing authorization for nirsevimab in August 2024, enabling private-sector availability.
- Its single-dose strategy substantially reduces the healthcare utilization burden and complements existing neonatal frameworks like Kangaroo Mother Care and Family Integrated Care in resource-limited settings.
