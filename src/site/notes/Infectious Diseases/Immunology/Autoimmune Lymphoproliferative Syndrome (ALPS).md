## Introduction And Pathogenesis

- Autoimmune Lymphoproliferative Syndrome is a primary immune regulatory disorder.
- It is alternatively known as Canale-Smith syndrome.
- The disease fundamentally arises from an underlying disorder of abnormal lymphocyte apoptosis.

### Genetic Defect

- The genetic deficit predominantly involves germline or somatic pathogenic variants in the **_FAS_ gene.**
- The _FAS_ gene encodes a cell surface receptor belonging to the tumor necrosis factor receptor superfamily.
- Less frequently, the condition is caused by pathogenic variants in other Fas pathway genes.
- These alternative gene variants specifically include **_FASLG_ and _CASP10_.**
- Targeted gene sequencing is strictly necessary to establish this genetic diagnosis.
- Genetic sequencing is crucial because standard functional immunological testing is frequently normal in immune regulatory disorders.

### Cellular Mechanisms

- Under normal physiologic conditions, stimulation of the FAS receptor induces programmed cell death.
- This process safely clears activated lymphocytes after an immune response.
- The genetic variants prevent this normal apoptotic clearance.
- The failure of lymphocyte apoptosis leads to the persistent survival of activated lymphocytes.
- This abnormal survival directly causes severe immune dysregulation and widespread autoimmunity.
- A cardinal cellular hallmark is the accumulation of polyclonal double-negative T cells.
- Double-negative T cells characteristically express CD3 and alpha/beta antigen receptors.
- However, they distinctly lack both CD4 and CD8 co-receptors.
- These double-negative T cells respond very poorly to antigens and fail to produce interleukin-2.

## Clinical Manifestations

- The disease typically presents very early in life.
- Initial presentation usually occurs in the first year of life.
- The vast majority of patients become highly symptomatic by 5 years of age.

### Lymphoproliferative Features

- A defining clinical feature is massive, chronic, and persistent nonmalignant lymphadenopathy.
- Massive splenomegaly is consistently observed in affected children.
- This massive splenomegaly frequently produces clinical hypersplenism.
- Hepatomegaly is additionally present in approximately half of all patients.
- The pronounced lymphoproliferative process may regress slowly over time.

### Autoimmune And Malignant Complications

- Unlike the lymphoproliferation, the associated autoimmunity typically does not regress.
- Autoimmune manifestations are characterized by frequent, severe exacerbations.
- Autoimmunity predominantly targets hematopoietic cells.
- This targeting produces severe multilineage cytopenias.
- Common manifestations include Coombs-positive autoimmune hemolytic anemia and immune thrombocytopenia.
- The concurrent presentation of immune thrombocytopenia and autoimmune hemolytic anemia is known as Evans syndrome.
- Patients frequently present with mild autoimmune neutropenia.
- Diverse systemic autoimmune manifestations can also occur.
- These include uveitis, glomerulonephritis, hepatitis, and central nervous system involvement like Guillain-Barré syndrome.
- Patients harbor a lifelong, significantly elevated risk for developing malignancies.
- The risk is particularly high for Hodgkin and non-Hodgkin lymphomas.

## Diagnostic Criteria

The formal diagnosis relies on a specific revised set of clinical and laboratory criteria. A complete blood count and flow cytometry are essential initial diagnostic steps.

### Diagnostic Categories And Thresholds

| Criteria Type                    | Specific Clinical And Laboratory Findings                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Required Criteria**            | ➤ Chronic, nonmalignant, noninfectious lymphadenopathy and/or splenomegaly persisting for over 6 months.<br>➤ Elevated double-negative T cells comprising $\ge$ 1.5% of total lymphocytes or 2.5% of CD3+ lymphocytes.                                                                                                                              |
| **Primary Accessory Criteria**   | ➤ Demonstration of defective lymphocyte apoptosis in two separate assays.<br>➤ Identification of a pathogenic somatic or germline mutation in FAS, FASLG, or CASP10.                                                                                                                                                                                |
| **Secondary Accessory Criteria** | ➤ Elevated specific plasma biomarkers: sFasL (>200 pg/mL), interleukin-10 (>20 pg/mL), vitamin B12 (>1500 ng/L), or interleukin-18 (>500 pg/mL).<br>➤ Typical immunohistologic findings on biopsy.<br>➤ Presence of autoimmune cytopenias combined with polyclonal hypergammaglobulinemia.<br>➤ Family history of nonmalignant lymphoproliferation. |

### Diagnostic Confirmation

- A definitive diagnosis is securely established by the presence of both required criteria plus one primary accessory criterion.
- A probable diagnosis is established by the presence of both required criteria plus one secondary accessory criterion.

## Management

- Medical management primarily targets the control of severe adenopathy and autoimmune cytopenias.
- Immunomodulatory therapy is the cornerstone of treatment.
- Mycophenolate mofetil is heavily utilized to control disease activity.
- Mammalian target of rapamycin inhibitors are also highly effective.
- Sirolimus is the preferred mammalian target of rapamycin inhibitor utilized in this clinical setting.
