## Introduction

- Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome is a profound combined immunodeficiency disorder.
- It is inherited exclusively in an X-linked recessive pattern.
- The disease classically presents with a recognized clinical triad.
- This triad includes severe 
	- atopic dermatitis, 
	- congenital thrombocytopenia, and 
	- an increased susceptibility to recurrent infections.

## Pathogenesis And Genetics

- The condition is caused by pathogenic variants in the _WASP_ gene.
- This gene encodes the Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome protein.
- The Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome protein plays a fundamental role in controlling the assembly of actin filaments.
- Normal actin filament assembly is critically required for normal cell migration and effective cell-to-cell interactions.
- The underlying immunologic deficit stems directly from the inability of T cells to provide adequate help to B cells.
- Under electron microscopy, T cells in affected patients uniquely lack the markedly fimbriated surface that is characteristic of normal T cells.
## Clinical Manifestations

### Hematological And Bleeding Manifestations

- Bleeding manifestations typically present very early in infancy.
- Patients frequently present with prolonged bleeding from the circumcision site.
- Bloody diarrhea and widespread petechiae are commonly observed early signs.
- The bleeding diathesis is driven by a congenital thrombocytopenia characterized by structurally defective platelets.

### Infectious Susceptibilities

- Patients exhibit a marked susceptibility to recurrent pyogenic infections.
- Typical early infections include severe otitis media, pneumonia, meningitis, and sepsis.
- These pyogenic infections are primarily driven by encapsulated bacteria such as _Streptococcus pneumoniae_ and _Haemophilus influenzae_.
- Later in the disease course, patients experience a higher frequency of infections with opportunistic organisms.
- These opportunistic pathogens frequently include _Pneumocystis jirovecii_ and viruses belonging to the herpesvirus family.

### Dermatologic And Systemic Features

- Severe atopic dermatitis, or eczema, is a hallmark feature of the disease.
- Physical examination frequently reveals prominent hepatosplenomegaly and widespread lymphadenopathy.

### Autoimmune And Malignant Complications

- Affected individuals harbor a highly significant risk of developing autoimmune conditions.
- There is a marked predisposition to developing autoimmune hemolytic anemia later in childhood.
- Patients possess a profoundly elevated risk of developing malignancies.
- Epstein-Barr virus (EBV)-associated malignancies and lymphoreticular cancers are particularly prominent.

## Laboratory Diagnosis

### Hematological Profile

- A complete blood count reveals the hallmark finding of significant thrombocytopenia.
- Platelet count and mean platelet volume evaluation are vital diagnostic steps.
- The thrombocytopenia is distinctively characterized by structurally small, defective platelets.
- Peripheral blood eosinophilia is a very common laboratory finding.

### Immunological And Humoral Assessment

- The classic serum immunoglobulin profile is highly characteristic of the disease.
- Patients exhibit a profound impairment in their humoral immune response to polysaccharide antigens.
- This impairment is evidenced by absent or greatly diminished natural isohemagglutinins.
- Patients demonstrate exceptionally poor antibody responses following immunization with polysaccharide vaccines.

|Immunoglobulin|Expected Finding in Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome|
|:--|:--|
|**IgM**|Low level.|
|**IgA**|Elevated level.|
|**IgE**|Elevated level.|
|**IgG**|Normal or slightly decreased level.|

### Cellular Immunity Evaluation

- Evaluation of cellular immunity typically demonstrates overall lymphopenia.
- Flow cytometry reveals moderately reduced percentages of circulating T cells.
- Lymphocyte proliferative responses to in vitro mitogen stimulation are variably depressed.

Careful differentiation from other primary immune deficiency diseases with overlapping features is necessary.

## Management

### Supportive And Medical Care

- General supportive care requires appropriate nutrition and the aggressive management of eczema.
- Any associated cutaneous infections must be managed promptly and aggressively.
- Patients require lifelong immunoglobulin replacement therapy to correct the profound functional antibody deficiency.
- Immunoglobulin therapy must be maintained regardless of the specific serum immunoglobulin isotype levels.

### Antimicrobial Prophylaxis And Vaccination

- Continuous antimicrobial prophylaxis is frequently recommended to prevent life-threatening opportunistic infections.
- This prophylaxis specifically targets _Pneumocystis jirovecii_ and herpes simplex virus.
- Patients must only be administered killed vaccines.
- Live-attenuated vaccines pose a severe infection risk in this specific immunocompromised state and are strictly contraindicated.

### Surgical Interventions

- Splenectomy may become a necessary intervention for patients experiencing severe, refractory thrombocytopenia.
- Following a splenectomy, patients require mandatory lifelong penicillin prophylaxis.
- Penicillin prophylaxis is vital to protect against overwhelming sepsis from encapsulated organisms.

### Curative And Advanced Therapies

- Allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT) remains the definitive treatment of choice.
- The procedure is usually curative when a high-quality matched donor is available.
- Patient survival approaches 100 percent when HSCT is performed utilizing a matched sibling donor.
- Ex vivo gene transfer to autologous hematopoietic stem cells serves as an alternative advanced therapy.
- Gene therapy utilizing modern lentiviral vectors has demonstrated clinical recovery and long-term immune reconstitution.
- Early gene therapy trials utilizing older retroviral vectors were significantly complicated by insertional mutagenesis leading to malignancy.

## Prenatal Diagnosis

- Prenatal diagnosis is strictly indicated in families with a previously affected child.
- Diagnosis can be performed utilizing chorionic villus sampling or fetal blood sampling.
- Evaluation focuses on identifying the specific _WAS_ mutation.
- Fetal blood sampling can also be utilized to document the presence of structurally small, low platelets in utero.
