## Introduction And Pathogenesis

- Chronic Granulomatous Disease is a rare primary immunodeficiency.
- Phagocytes display normal chemotaxis, ingestion, and degranulation capabilities.
- They completely fail to kill catalase-positive microorganisms.
- The core defect resides within the phagocyte nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate (NADPH) oxidase enzyme complex.
- This structural defect prevents the vital respiratory burst.
- It halts the normal generation of microbicidal reactive oxygen species such as superoxide and hydrogen peroxide.
- The phagocytic vacuoles remain abnormally acidic.
- Ingested bacteria successfully survive inside the cells.
- This continuous pathogen survival induces the formation of exuberant inflammatory granulomas.

## Clinical Manifestations

- Clinical signs characteristically begin in early infancy.

### Infectious Features

- Patients develop recurrent, deep-seated, and indolent infections.
- Classic presentations include recurrent pneumonia and suppurative lymphadenitis.
- Deep hepatic abscesses, subcutaneous abscesses, and multilocular osteomyelitis frequently occur.
- The disease is uniquely defined by a marked susceptibility to specific catalase-positive organisms.
- _Staphylococcus aureus_ is the most common offending bacterial pathogen.
- Other sentinel bacterial organisms include _Burkholderia cepacia_, _Serratia marcescens_, _Nocardia_, and _Salmonella_.
- Patients are highly susceptible to opportunistic fungal organisms like _Aspergillus_ and _Candida albicans_.

### Non-Infectious Inflammatory Features

- Inflammatory granuloma formation is a prominent clinical feature.
- Granulomas frequently cause severe mechanical obstruction of the gastric outlet.
- This can lead to overt pyloric obstruction.
- Granulomas can also obstruct the urinary tract (ureters) and the biliary tree.
- Over 80% of patients exhibit positive serology for Crohn disease.
- They frequently develop a chronic granulomatous colitis or enteritis that closely mimics Crohn disease.
- Other sequelae include anemia of chronic disease, poor growth, chronic purulent dermatitis, and restrictive lung disease.

## Laboratory Diagnosis

### Functional Assays

- The dihydrorhodamine (DHR) reduction assay via flow cytometry is the primary diagnostic test.
- It evaluates phagocyte oxidant production through increased fluorescence upon DHR oxidation by hydrogen peroxide.
- The DHR assay is highly sensitive.
- It has largely replaced the older nitroblue tetrazolium (NBT) slide dye test.
- It successfully identifies the X-linked carrier state in mothers by demonstrating a characteristic bimodal response to neutrophil stimulation.
- Severe myeloperoxidase (MPO) deficiency can cause a falsely positive DHR result for neutrophils.
- In MPO deficiency, eosinophils will still reduce DHR normally, safely differentiating it from Chronic Granulomatous Disease.

### Confirmatory And Surrogate Testing

|Investigation|Role in Evaluation|
|:--|:--|
|**Genetic Testing**|Targeted DNA sequencing definitively confirms the specific genetic subgroup.|
|**Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)**|Crucial surrogate marker for detecting clinically silent deep tissue infections.|
|**Prenatal Diagnosis**|Fetal blood sampling can be utilized with DHR flow cytometry for previously affected families.|

- The ESR should be monitored regularly when patients are well and whenever they appear ill.
- Deep-seated infections often yield negative cultures.
- A downward trend in ESR indicates a positive response to antimicrobial therapy.
- A failure of ESR to decrease suggests the need to broaden coverage, such as adding antifungals.

## Management

### Medical Prophylaxis

- Patients require lifelong continuous antimicrobial prophylaxis.
- Daily oral trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX) is heavily utilized.
- It significantly reduces the overall frequency of bacterial infections.
- Itraconazole is routinely employed to prevent invasive fungal infections, particularly _Aspergillus_.
- Prophylactic administration of subcutaneous interferon-gamma is strongly recommended.
- It significantly decreases the frequency of hospitalizations and severe infections.

### Acute Treatment And Curative Therapy

- Acute infections mandate aggressive and prolonged courses of parenteral antibiotics.
- Surgical drainage of deep abscesses is frequently necessary for both therapeutic and diagnostic utility.
- Short pulses of systemic corticosteroids are effectively utilized to manage obstructive granulomatous complications in the lungs, urinary tract, or gastrointestinal tract.
- Allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT) remains the only definitive, curative therapy.
- It is strongly recommended early in life when a suitable sibling or unrelated donor is available.
- Non-malignant primary immunodeficiencies successfully cured by HSCT include Chronic Granulomatous Disease.
