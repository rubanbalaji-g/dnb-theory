**Ultrasound is the primary tool for detecting prenatal anomalies, allowing early identification of structural malformations, guiding further investigations, and enabling timely counseling and management.**

### Principles of Ultrasound in Prenatal Anomaly Detection

- **Safe, non-invasive, real-time imaging** using high-frequency sound waves.
- Provides detailed information on **fetal anatomy, growth, position, and amniotic fluid**.
- Can be repeated multiple times during pregnancy without radiation risk.
### Timing of Key Scans

- **First-trimester scan (11–13 weeks):** <span style="color:rgb(255, 255, 0)">DATING SCAN</span>
    - Detects major anomalies like acrania, holoprosencephaly, large encephalocele, ectopia cordis, exomphalos, gastroschisis, body stalk anomaly1.
    - Early detection of **open spina bifida** and **major cardiac defects** is possible with improved protocols1.
        
- **Second-trimester anomaly scan (18–22 weeks):** <span style="color:rgb(255, 255, 0)">ANOMOLY SCAN</span>
    - Gold standard for **systematic evaluation of fetal organs**
    - Detects CNS malformations, cardiac defects, facial clefts, abdominal wall defects, renal anomalies, skeletal dysplasias.
    - Uses **four-chamber view of heart, outflow tracts, mid-sagittal brain sections, and facial views** for comprehensive assessment1.
        
- **Third-trimester scan (28–40 weeks):** <span style="color:rgb(255, 255, 0)">GROWTH SCAN</span>
    - Monitors growth restriction, late-onset anomalies, and evolving conditions (e.g., hydrocephalus, bowel obstruction).
### Advances in Ultrasound Technology

- **High-resolution transducers** improve visualization of subtle defects.  
- **Color Doppler** enhances cardiac evaluation.
- **3D/4D ultrasound** provides spatial views of face, limbs, and spine.
- **Spatiotemporal Image Correlation (STIC)** and **Fetal HQ** improve fetal echocardiography1.
- **Artificial intelligence integration** is emerging to enhance detection rates
### Limitations

- Detection rates vary with **gestational age, maternal habitus, fetal position, and operator expertise**.
- Some anomalies (e.g., metabolic disorders, mild structural defects) may not be detected prenatally.
- False positives can occur, requiring correlation with **genetic testing (amniocentesis, chorionic villus sampling)** or **MRI**.
### Notes on Key Prenatal Ultrasound Scans

- **Dating Scan (11–14 weeks):**
    - Confirms gestational age by measuring crown-rump length (CRL).
    - Assesses viability and number of fetuses.
    - Screens for major early anomalies and nuchal translucency for chromosomal risk.
        
- **Anomaly Scan (18–22 weeks):**
    - Detailed systematic evaluation of fetal anatomy.
    - Detects structural anomalies in brain, heart, face, abdomen, limbs, and spine.
    - Assesses placenta, amniotic fluid, and fetal growth parameters.
        
- **Growth Scans (Third trimester, 28–40 weeks):**
    - Monitor fetal growth and well-being.
    - Detects intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR) or macrosomia.
    - Evaluates amniotic fluid volume and Doppler studies for fetal circulation.
